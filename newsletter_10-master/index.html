ECI Newsletter 10: Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Tunnel

January 2022

Background

Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Tunnel, the first underwater tunnel in South Asia. Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Tunnel, in short, Bangabandhu Tunnel or Karnaphuli Tunnel, is an under-construction underwater expressway tunnel in the port city of Chattogram, Bangladesh under the Karnaphuli River. The length of the main tunnel is 3.32km, with each tube measuring 2.45km at a diameter of 10.80 metres. Each tube will have two lanes, with connecting roads for the main tunnel and a 727-metre over-bridge. Located at the river mouth in Chittagong, the tunnel is 9.3km long, in which the length of the tunnel shield section is 2,450m while the tunnel outer diameter is 11.8m. The 3,400-meter-long tunnel project is being constructed at a cost of around Tk 10,000 crore which will be open to public and vehicles by December 2022.


Chittagong, located at the southeast coast of Bangladesh, is the main sea port and largest commercial city of the country. Most of the export and import activities of the country are being carried out through Chittagong Port situated in the mouth of the Karnaphuli River. The Karnaphuli divides Chittagong City into two parts. The northwest part is confined to the city and the port and the southeast part is the area of heavy industries. Current two bridges are not sufficient to accommodate the existing and increasing huge traffic flow. Due to river morphology, siltation on the river bed of the Karnaphuli is a major problem and the threat for proper functioning of the Chittagong Port. To face the problem of siltation, Bangladesh Bridge Authority (BBA) intends to construct a multi-lane road tunnel crossing the Karnaphuli river instead of construction of another bridge over the same river.

Bangladesh Bridge Authority was entrusted to implement the project. China Communication Construction Company Ltd. jointly ventured with Ove Arup and Partners Hong Kong Ltd. (CCCC-Arup JV, the Consultant) was awarded to implement the project. The DeVCon (a national consulting firm) has been engaged as the nominated sub-consultant to support the main consultants JV in some specific fields including traffic study, engineering survey including topographic survey, underwater/bathymetric survey, and geological and geotechnical Investigations, social and environmental study with preparation of socioeconomic report, Resettlement Action Plan (RAP), Environmental Impact Assessment.


Project Name: Karnaphuli Tunnel.

Project Cost: $1.2 billion.

Project Location: Chittagong, Karnaphuli River.

Geotechnical Point of view of Karnaphuli Tunnel

Chittagong is the major port city and the gateway of Bangladesh. It is also the second largest city and the“commercial capital” of the country. The Karnaphuli River divides Chittagong City into two parts. One part is confined to the city and the port, the other part is the area of heavy industry.

Geologically the area falls within the Bengal Foredeep of Bengal Basin where Neogene sediments with alternation of shale and sandstone are well developed. The general elevation of the investigated area ranges from -8.8 to 4.395 m. Due to river morphology, siltation on the bed of the Karnaphuli River is a big problem and the major threat for proper functioning of the Chittagong Port. To face the problem of siltation, Bangladesh Bridge Authority (BBA) intends to construct a tunnel crossing the Karnaphuli River instead of construction of another bridge over the same river.


Physical Description

Seven (7) boreholes were drilled along the proposed tunnel alignment. Boreholes were arranged along the proposed tunnel axis on both sides of the river. Borehole locations were considered at an interval of 300-500m with simple engineering geological conditions while at an interval of 100-300m with complicated engineering geological conditions.


Delivering for the community

The Karnaphuli Tunnel is a game changer for the southeast region of Bangladesh, presenting a solution to connectivity issues to the rest of the country. Upon its completion in 2022, the tunnel will form another link in the connection between North and Southeast Asia. It is also part of China’s One Belt One Road initiative (OBOR) and a key link in planned cross-country transport corridors.

Along with the Chittagong Outer Ring Road and the Dhaka Chattogram Expressway – both of which were designed by SMEC – the Karnaphuli Tunnel is poised to be a catalyst for the economic transformation of southeast Bangladesh into an industrial hub. The economy of Bangladesh will receive a significant boost once the tunnel is constructed, creating major employment opportunities for the region.


Opportunities

A mega construction of new roads, approach roads, industries and tourism facilities are on their triumphant march centering the much-hyped Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman Tunnel aiming to transform the landscape of the port city.

The Bangladesh Bridge Authority (BBA) says the tunnel will connect Anwara with the port city and reduce 50-kilometre distance from the capital Dhaka and 15-kilometre from Chattogram to tourism capital Cox’s Bazar. It will connect Anwara to Patenga near Shah Amanat International Airport and Chattogram Port in the metropolis as well.

The government plans to expand the port city on both sides of the river as “One City,Two-Town Model” much like Shanghai in China.

The underground passageway is a significant part of the world longest Super Marine Drive from Mirsrai to Teknaf and Asian Highway connecting Chttogram port and Matarbari deep seaport.


Future Plan

A new city in the offing on southern Karnaphuli

With the construction of the tunnel, the Anwara-Karnaphuli area will take a new look. From the Anwara intersection of the tunnel and from the area adjacent to Parki sea beach to Shah Amanat Bridge under the Karnaphuli upazila area, a 12-kilometre-long area along the bank of Karnaphuli River witnesses a series of construction work by government, private and foreign agencies.

A city plan is underway

The industrial hub Chattogram stands on the north side of Karnaphuli River that generates 40 percent of industrial production which is the gateway to 80 percent of international trade and 50 percent of revenue collection.

With an almost 2.25 percent yearly average urban population growth, the city will be a home to seven million people by 2035, according to the United Nations Urbanisation Prospect report 2018.

Socio-Economic Development

Dr Atiur Rahman, former governor of Bangladesh Bank and eminent economist, told the Bangladesh Post, “Tunnels are more cost-effective than bridges. This will reduce the cost of travel both for individual and commercial vehicles. The value addition to the economy can be substantial. I haven’t calculated the value addition, but there will be huge gains in terms of GDP,” he added.

The economists said the special economic zones in the region will also benefit hugely from the tunnel in terms of better forward and backward linkages.

“While these mega infrastructures will take the country to a new trajectory of higher growth, the challenges remain: who will run them? The quality of human resources will have to be vastly improved by injecting more skills into it,” he said.

Atiur suggested restructuring of the education system to produce quality human resources which can take advantage of these infrastructures and make the economy more productive. “The productivity gains must be at the heart of our drive for a new trajectory of growth supported by these mega infrastructures,” he said.

At last, The tunnel will greatly improve the development of the regional economy in Bangladesh as well as play an important role in interconnectivity between Bangladesh and its neighboring countries.
